Iron Heart

73º59'14"W 40º40'0"N

15:20 09 Mar 2019

Today we went out to suburban Tokyo. A town called Hachioji. This hour long train ride, followed by a 30 minute bus ride was for one purpose; Iron Heart Japan. Iron Heart is a Japanese clothing company focused on long lasting tough clothes. Shinichi Haraki creates Iron Heat in 2003 aimed at the motorcycling community. The clothing style is what I think of as 1950’s Americana, and the inside of the shop reflected that aesthetic.

Inside

Inside

From the moment we arrived the staff was kind and very eager to help. They were patient and accommodating.

Inside

After I picked out the jeans I wanted, they measured the inseam and took them to hem with a chain stitch sewing machine.

My new jeans getting a chain stitch hem

We were lucky enough to be there while Harakisan was visiting the shop so we were able to get a picture with him!

Harakisan

We went home with a lot of new clothes. Ashley even got her first Iron Heart gear!

Our Haul

They asked us about our trip and we’re excited to hear we were on our honeymoon and when we checked out they threw in many gifts including stickers, patches, and drawstring denim bags. After we left the shop on a high from how fun the visit was, they chased us down and asked to take a picture of us in front of the shop for their blog!

Shibuya Pt. 2

Our first full day in Tokyo was a lot of fun. Despite the rain we had a great time checking out a lot of shops in Shibuya including my long planned denim tour.

We started out the day going to About Life coffee brewers. A really cute shop that had really incredible cofee.

About Life Coffee Brewers

About Life Coffee Brewers

It was raining pretty hard at this point and I was a bit paranoid about my camera so we got a cheap umbrella at FamilyMart which is a very popular convenience store here. After grabbing that we headed to Don Quijote which is the "everything" store. It’s six floors, plus a basement that has pretty much everything you can imagine. We found stationary, pens, snacks, drinks (including a peach Coca-cola), and tons of other stuff. The best way that I can illustrate the full range of products they had is to say that after we walked through the shoe area, which had everything from cheap Payless style shoes to Nike AirMax, we walked pass the watch area that had a $25,000 Rolex watch which was about 15ft away from cheap bedroom slippers.

Don Quijote haul

We mostly got Japanese drug store type things from there, including some crazy cooling eye drops that are really refreshing. From there we went to Tokyo Hands which is a six floor crafts store. I was mostly looking for pens. I found a really nice embossed Pilot pen, and Ashley got a nice notebook to document our trip and a new planner!

After Tokyo hands we started our Tokyo denim tour. The first stop was Studio D'Artisan. I have a pair of thier jeans that you can see at the bottom of my packing list post.

Studio D’Artisan

Next up was Yoshida Porter which is a really nice Japan made bag and luggage brand. Sadly they were closed so we moved on to Pure Blue Japan where I got a really nice pair of Jeans. We stopped by The Flat Head, but they didn't have anything that really jumped out at me so we walked back to Porter where I got a nice little coin case zipper wallet.

Denim tour haul pt. 1

On the outside you can see the two Stuido shirts I got. In the middle is a Denim flag for Studio D'Artisan that I may frame when we get back, and in the middle on the bottom are the Pure Blue Japan 22oz-013 that I got!

At this point we were pretty hungry so we stopped for something to eat. We went to a little cafe called Brown Rice that served an all vegan menu.

Inside Brown Rice

Seasonal menu

The food was amazing!! From top left to bottom right; a potato salad, ginger deep fried tofu with veggies, tempura fried gobu, brown rice of course, pickled raddish, and miso soup. We may need to go back!

Shibuya walk home

From there we walked back to our Airbnb with the intention of putting our purchases down and getting back out but we ended up passing out. The time different caught up with us I think. I'm writting this Friday morning as we get ready to head out. We're going to the Iron Heart store in suburban Tokyo, and then returning to city center to checkout the imperial palace. See you tomorrow!

Shibuya

73º59'14"W 40º40'0"N

06:43 07 Mar 2019

We made it! We're in Tokyo! We only had enough energy to walk around for an hour or two before getting some rest but so far is incredible. And a little overwhelming. I thought; "I live in NYC, and I've been to a lot of big cities. How different could it be?" but it's a bit of a sensory assualt. I'm sure how tired I was from the flight was a factor.

Crossing near the 109 Building in Shibuya

The flight was really easy. We got pretty lucky and had a row of three seats for just the two of us. Which meant our bags could mostly fit under the middle seat and we each had plenty of leg room. In addition to that our seats were only seperated by a closet for the bathroom. So it was easy to get up as much as we needed to to stretch and walk a bit. There was a service gally close to our seats with "help yourself" snacks, ice lemon water, and coffee. I’m not sure if it was excitement or caffeine but I didn’t sleep a wink on the 14 hour flight. I just watched Seinfeld and played Zelda on my Switch. Lastly the food was actually really good. It was a lot of food and wasn’t the very basic snacks I’m used to for vegan options.

When we landed in Tokyo we had zero trouble getting through immigration and customs. Maybe 10 minutes total for the two. After that we exchanged our Japan Rail pass voucher for the actual pass and got tickets for the train to Shibuya. I also got a Suica card with my name on it!

Personalized Suica card

Even just the train to Shibuya kind of blew my mind. First of all different kinds of trains stop at the same platform. A JR East train came through right before the NEX train. And even crazier to me was that the trains used the same platform in different directions. And even more incredible to me was that the NEX train split! All twelve cars went from Narita to Tokyo station, but at that point, the first six cars went to one destination, while the back six cars continued to the Shinjuku destination.

The view from our Airbnb

We stepped into a drug store to try and grab some body wash. There were four packed floors and we only found pretty large size bottles of body wash. We started to get a bit overwhelmed by the crowd, so we stepped outside and I think it was actually more crowded out there. We just grabbed some snacks and headed back to the Airbnb to get some rest before our first full day here!

I like over optimizing packing and traveling as light as possible. On my trip to Japan I plan to carry just this backpack. I'll caviat that by saying I do plan to buy a lot while I’m there. Including another bag to fill with things to bring home. But leaving New York, this is all I’ll have with me.

Mission Workshop R6 Arkiv Pack

This is my Mission Workshop R6 Arkiv Pack (22L). I'm using the two of the vertical side zip attachments, one on each side, the Axis waist pack is attached to the front on top, and the front bottom attachment is actually a GoRuck GR1 field pocket using Mission Workshop's Arkiv clips to attach using the molle straps.

Clothes

We're staying at Airbnb's on our trip so I can travel really light when it comes to clothes. I'm taking four boxer briefs, four pairs of socks, one gym shirt, running shorts, and two plain cotton tees. I'm also carrying a small clothes line because I don't like to dry my shirts and risk shrinking them. It took a long time to find my favorite plain tees but the 3sixteen heavyweight tee is my go-to.

Packed

Cables

The top left is a small battery bank by Anker. It has two USB out ports, and has built in prongs to plug right into the wall. I'm also bringing an HDMI cable and adapter to put The Office or Seinfeld on the TV where we're staying.

Camera gear

My camera gear is pretty light. I shoot on a Fujifilm X-T2 using their 35mm lens. Because of the APS-C sensor the lens is an effective 50mm but that's a good length for how I shoot. I'm using Peak Design's strap system, and a Pelican SD card case.

Packed camera gear

This is Peak Design's Wash Pack. It's, of course, a great dopp kit bag, but for travel I want to carry as little as possible when it comes to toiletries. Thanks to the organization in this pouch, It makes a great camera pack. All my camera gear is packed and safe in here.

Toiletries

Toiletries packed

Main compartment items

This is everything going into the main compartment of the bag. Additional items not shown before; The red bag is holding a compressed travel pillow. The bottom left is a packed Osprey daypack. In the middle is a rolled up dry bag to hold dirty laundry.

Travel docs

Other quick access cables

Electronics

iPad Pro 11 with the keyboard case, Nintendo Switch with a couple of games, Airpods, Shure 315's IEMs for the flight, and the Bose SoundLink Micro for music and podcasts in the Airbnb.

Wearing

Finally, here are the things I plan to wear. Including Iron Heart heavyweight hoodie, Iron Heart flannel, and Studio D'Artisan SD-108's, and Buzz Rickson's mil-spec sneakers.

I don't leave for another few days but I'm so excited I've been packing for over a week. I truely can't wait!
