I like over optimizing packing and traveling as light as possible. On my trip to Japan I plan to carry just this backpack. I'll caviat that by saying I do plan to buy a lot while I’m there. Including another bag to fill with things to bring home. But leaving New York, this is all I’ll have with me.

Mission Workshop R6 Arkiv Pack

This is my Mission Workshop R6 Arkiv Pack (22L). I'm using the two of the vertical side zip attachments, one on each side, the Axis waist pack is attached to the front on top, and the front bottom attachment is actually a GoRuck GR1 field pocket using Mission Workshop's Arkiv clips to attach using the molle straps.

Clothes

We're staying at Airbnb's on our trip so I can travel really light when it comes to clothes. I'm taking four boxer briefs, four pairs of socks, one gym shirt, running shorts, and two plain cotton tees. I'm also carrying a small clothes line because I don't like to dry my shirts and risk shrinking them. It took a long time to find my favorite plain tees but the 3sixteen heavyweight tee is my go-to.

Packed

Cables

The top left is a small battery bank by Anker. It has two USB out ports, and has built in prongs to plug right into the wall. I'm also bringing an HDMI cable and adapter to put The Office or Seinfeld on the TV where we're staying.

Camera gear

My camera gear is pretty light. I shoot on a Fujifilm X-T2 using their 35mm lens. Because of the APS-C sensor the lens is an effective 50mm but that's a good length for how I shoot. I'm using Peak Design's strap system, and a Pelican SD card case.

Packed camera gear

This is Peak Design's Wash Pack. It's, of course, a great dopp kit bag, but for travel I want to carry as little as possible when it comes to toiletries. Thanks to the organization in this pouch, It makes a great camera pack. All my camera gear is packed and safe in here.

Toiletries packed

Main compartment items

This is everything going into the main compartment of the bag. Additional items not shown before; The red bag is holding a compressed travel pillow. The bottom left is a packed Osprey daypack. In the middle is a rolled up dry bag to hold dirty laundry.

Electronics

iPad Pro 11 with the keyboard case, Nintendo Switch with a couple of games, Airpods, Shure 315's IEMs for the flight, and the Bose SoundLink Micro for music and podcasts in the Airbnb.

Wearing

Finally, here are the things I plan to wear. Including Iron Heart heavyweight hoodie, Iron Heart flannel, and Studio D'Artisan SD-108's, and Buzz Rickson's mil-spec sneakers.

I don't leave for another few days but I'm so excited I've been packing for over a week. I truely can't wait!
